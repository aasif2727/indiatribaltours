Payments

PAYMENTS:-

For all the services contracted, certain advance payment should be made to hold the booking, on confirmed basis & the balance amount can be paid either before your departure from your country or upon arrival in INDIA but definitely before the commencement of the services. Management personnel hold the right to decide upon the amount to be paid as advance payment, based on the nature of the service & the time left for the commencement of the service.

Apart from above in some cases like Special Train Journeys, hotels or resorts bookings during the peak season (X-Mas, New Year), full payment is required to be sent in advance.

MODE OF PAYMENT:-

Overseas advance payment can be made through Wire Transfer to our bank .

CANCELLATION POLICY:-

In the event of cancellation of tour / travel services due to any avoidable / unavoidable reason/s we must be notified of the same in writing. Cancellation charges will be effective from the date we receive advice in writing, and cancellation charges would be as follows: -

25% of the tour package cost per cancelled passenger (30 days prior to arrival)

50% of the tour package cost per cancelled passenger (29 to 15 days prior to arrival)

100% of the tour package cost per cancelled passenger (14 Days prior to arrival)

In case of Special Trains Journeys and peak season hotel bookings a separate cancellation policy is applicable (which can be advised as and when required).

In case you cancel the trip after commencement, refund would be restricted to a limited amount only which too would depend on the amount that we would be able to recover from the hoteliers/ contractors we patronize. For unused hotel accommodation, chartered transportation & missed meals etc. we do not bear any responsibility to refund.

COVID-19 Travel guideline

During Travel

During the journey, announcements regarding COVID-19 precautionary measures should be made at the airport and on the plane.

The in-flight crew needs to make sure that COVID-19 appropriate behaviour is being followed.

If a passenger displays signs of COVID-19 during the journey, he will be isolated as per the protocols.

Announcements regarding testing requirements and travellers who need to undergo the exam to avoid crowding at the arrival airport should be made at the airlines.

On Arrival

The passengers need to de-board, ensuring physical distancing. At the airport, the health authorities will request to see the self-declaration form and carry out the laid down protocol

If any passenger shows symptoms during the screening, they will be isolated as per the laid down guidelines. If they tests positive, their contacts shall be identified and managed according to the laid down protocol.

2% of the passengers will be randomly tested at the arrival airport, after which they will be allowed to leave. The samples will be further sent for genomic testing if they test positive. The COVID-19 treatment for such flyers will be provided according to the latest/ updated standard protocol.

Exemption has been granted to arriving G20 delegates at Indian Airports from 2% random testing for Covid-19.

Travel via Seaports

International passengers arriving through seaports/ land ports would undergo the same procedure as mentioned above, except the online registration facility is unavailable for such flyers at present.

These people must submit the self-declaration form to the authorities at the seaports/ land ports on arrival.

Privacy

Privacy Info

We're committed to providing you with the best information and inspiration to help you travel responsibly. We're also committed to respecting your privacy and looking after your data. This privacy policy explains what information we need from you, how and why we use it and the choices you have over the information you give us.

You don't need to give us any personal information in order to browse Responsible Travel. And we promise we never sell or lease your personal information to anyone else.

What information do we have?

Unless you request a brochure or leave your phone number, the only contact information we keep on our database are names and email addresses. If you decide to make an enquiry, we pass this on to the holiday company who runs the trip you're enquiring about. We might also ask for your phone number as this may help speed up the booking process for you.

We'll only ask for your postal address if you ask for a brochure or written materials from us. If you do give us your phone number and/or postal address we promise it will be automatically deleted after two months.

We also remember your previous enquiries and bookings and try to remember your preferences. This means we can recommend more relevant experiences for you next time you choose to use Responsible Travel. Because you don't make any bookings directly with Responsible Travel we don't have access to any of your payment or passport information.

So to clarify, the only personal contact information we hold about you is your name and email address, plus we remember some details of your past enquiries and bookings. This information is held on a secure server and our firewalls are regularly updated and can only be accessed by Responsible Travel staff or our IT supplier.
